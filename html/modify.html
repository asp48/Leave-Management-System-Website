About BMSCE


BMSCE

In the History of Karnataka, the name of Businayana Mukundadas  Sreenivasaiah [BMS] occupies a prominent place in the field of philanthropy. The Maharaja of Mysore honoured him with the title of Rajakarya Prasaktha in 1946.  He started the BMS College of Engineering in the same year.  He had foreseen the urgent need for high-quality technical education in India, even before independence.  The ideals, for which B.M.Sreenivasaiah stood, continues to inspire the inheritors of his legacy.

Sri.B.S.Narayan, the dynamic and enterprising son of the founder, took over the reins of the college after the sad demise of Sri.B.M.Sreenivasaiah. Under his able guidance, the BMS College of Engineering developed into one of the finest engineering colleges in the country.  He was also instrumental in initiating International Collaborative Programmes such as cross-cultural programmes with Melton Foundation, training foreign students under International Co-operation Division etc
